## Maintain BUY but lower TP to IDR2,500 from IDR2,900
Indosat will release 4Q25 results on 9 Feb’26; We estimate 4Q25 revenue of IDR14.4t (+2% YoY), net income of IDR1.4t (+31% YoY) and EBITDA of IDR6.8t (margin: 47%). We roll forward valuations to FY26E and maintain BUY with a lower TP of IDR2,500 (EV/EBITDA of 5.0x vs prior TP of IDR2,900). Our revised TP implies 15.6x P/E and 2.1x P/BV for FY26E.

## Momentum of year-end holiday and market repair
We forecast 4Q25 revenue of IDR14.4t (+2% YoY, +2% QoQ), taking FY25E revenue to IDR55.5t (-0.6% YoY). Mobile data likely continued as the growth driver, helped by the year-end holiday and signs of market repair; We estimate mobile data revenue of IDR11.4t (+4% YoY, +5% QoQ) and data traffic of 4,759 PB (+15% YoY, 5% QoQ), with data yield of IDR2,382/GB in 4Q25E. We estimate subscribers stabilised at 95m and 4Q25 ARPU of IDR42,700. Stricter activation rules should reduce unnecessary churn rate.

## We estimate 4Q25 net income of IDR1.4t
We forecast 4Q25 net income of IDR 1.4t, taking FY25E to IDR4.9t. This was mainly driven by cost efficiencies, with operating expenses projected at IDR 11.5t (-2% YoY, -1% QoQ), primarily reflecting controlled partnership and network costs. We expect 4Q25 EBITDA of IDR 6.8t (47.4% margin), bringing full-year EBITDA to IDR 26.5t (47.8% margin).

## Fibre optic divestment is an upside to our forecasts
We continue to favour Indosat due to its steady network expansion in Indonesia. We believe the ongoing market repair will sustain an improving competitive landscape, with the industry pivoting from price wars towards a greater focus on network quality and differentiation. Note that our current assumptions do not incorporate the potential divestment of fibre-optic assets (deal value of IDR14.6t: section 4). A successful transaction would represent meaningful upside to our dividend forecasts.

# 4. Plans to divest fibre optic assets

Indosat plans to divest its fibre-optic business by entering partnerships with Arsari Group (non-listed) and Northstar (non-listed). Indosat will retain about 45% of the stake, while the rest will be held by Arsari Group and Northstar. The deal value is about IDR 14.6 t. Based on media reports, Indosat owns about 86,000 km of fibre-optic cables; hence, we estimate the deal value at about IDR170m/km.

Indosat has not disclosed the financial information about the fibre optic business, but assuming 7.5x EV/EBITDA valuation and no debt, we estimate EBITDA of IDR2.0t and revenue of IDR3.9t (assuming a 50% EBITDA margin). Meanwhile, the average monthly price is subject to the number of core or bandwidth capacity.

## 5.2 Risks
There are several risks to our earnings forecasts, target price, and rating for ISAT. Key risks are economic slowdown and purchasing power, rising competition in network quality and product price, and lower margins (dominate by fixed costs).

# Value Proposition
*   ISAT has 135 MHz of spectrum, suitable for 5G.
*   ISAT operates 208,449 4G and 1,404 5G BTS in 3Q25.
*   The company will benefit from the adoption of digital lifestyle and a growing middle-class.

# Price Drivers
*   Merger with Hutchison 3 Indonesia.
*   Network transformation yielded higher financial performance.
*   ISAT lost 4m subscribers in 4Q24 - concern about price war.
*   Market repair as operator start to standardize new SIM Card price to IDR35,000/3GB.

# Financial Metrics
*   We forecast revenue to post a 2.6% FY24-27E CAGR to IDR60.3t by FY27E.
*   EBITDA is our key metric. We forecast EBITDA margin of 48% in FY25/26E.
*   We expect it to implement a lean business model in its expansion plan, especially on fixed broadband.

# Swing Factors

### Upside
*   Improving market competition, with price rationalization.
*   Better-than-expected cost efficiency post-merger.
*   Expansion in fixed-broadband with an asset-light business model.

### Downside
*   Competition among telco intensifies leading to a steep decline in data price.
*   Lower-than-expected data growth due to economic slowdown.
*   Surge in USD/IDR may affect network expansion.
*   A high-interest rate environment could increase its refinancing costs.
