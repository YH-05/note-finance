# Indosat Ooredoo Hutchison (ISAT IJ)

## Buy: 3Q mixed - mobile revenue up, but margins down

- 3Q results reaffirm mobile pricing discipline as ARPU rises 4% q/q, but EBITDA growth is muted at 1% q/q
- We expect earnings growth to improve with higher mobile revenue and growth in the enterprise segment
- Maintain Buy with unchanged TP of IDR3,000; key catalysts - mobile revenue growth and fibre asset monetisation

## Maintain Buy: earnings growth set to improve with higher mobile and enterprise revenue

We expect mobile revenue to rise due to higher mobile ARPU led by pricing discipline in the industry, removal of discounts and higher starter pack prices. Medium-term, we also expect the company to gain market share with the expansion of its network coverage and distribution, and increase its market share in the enterprise segment. Overall, we expect EBITDA / net profit CAGR of c5%/14% over 2024-27e. We see dividends rising progressively and expect the dividend payout ratio to rise to 70% by 2026e (from 55% in 2024). The stock is attractively valued at 2026e 3.7x EV/ EBITDA and 10.8x PE, with a 6.5% dividend yield. Our TP is unchanged at IDR3,000.

## 3Q25 revenue in line; ARPU up c4% q/q

Revenue for the quarter grew c4% q/q to IDR14trn. Mobile revenue was up c4% q/q to IDR11.8trn. This was a result of higher ARPU driven by higher starter pack prices, rationalisation of freebies and discounts, and hyper personalisation initiatives. Mobile ARPU for 3Q25 stood at IDR40k (vs IDR38.5k in 2Q). EBITDA for the quarter was up c1% q/q to IDR6.5trn. EBITDA growth was slower q/q due to some costs booked upfront in the enterprise segment and volatility around some cost items. EBITDA for 9M'25 came at IDR19.3trn (down 3% y/y) and was tracking at 71% our 2025 estimate. EBITDA margin for 9M25 stood at 47.0% (vs 47.8% for 9M24). Net profit for 3Q stood at IDR1.25trn up 9% y/y.

## Key highlights from the earnings call

1) The company reiterated its 2025 EBITDA growth guidance of low-single digit growth y/y. As per the company, 4Q'25 EBITDA would be driven by higher mobile revenue as consumer purchasing power is improving and pricing discipline is maintained in the industry. In addition, revenue contribution revenue from GPU-as-a-service to increase in 4Q and cost discipline would be maintained; 2) it expects mobile ARPU to trend upwards and expects ARPU to rise to IDR45-50k in the medium term; 3) the bulk of revenue from GPU-as-a-service for 2025 should be reflected in 4Q25. The company reiterated revenue from GPU as a service to be USD35m in 2025, with a 60% EBITDA margin; and 4) it expects fibre asset monetisation to conclude during 4Q25. With fibre being a strategic asset, particularly in the context of AI adoption, the company wants to balance short-term and long-term objectives and ensure long-term value creation through fibre asset monetisation.
