## 4Q25 Revenues of Rp15.4tn (+9.1% YoY, +9.3% QoQ)
Relatively in line with our estimate. Cellular revenue increased 8.4% YoY in 4Q25 vs. 1.5% YoY in 3Q25, while on a QoQ basis cellular revenue also increased 8.4%. Data revenue reversed its trend from a decline rate of 1.8% YoY in 3Q25 to an increase of 7.0% YoY in 4Q25, on the back of data traffic growth acceleration from 10.9% YoY in 3Q25 to 18.3% YoY in 4Q25. IOH saw its subscriber base declined 1.0% QoQ to 93.7mn in 4Q25, which we think could be due to low quality subs clean up. Meanwhile, blended ARPU increased 13.1% YoY/ 10.0% QoQ to Rp44.0k/month in 4Q25. Lastly, non-mobile revenues increased 12.5% YoY in 4Q25 as its MIDI and fixed revenue components increased 12.8% YoY and 10.0% YoY respectively in the same period. On a 12-month basis, IOH booked Rp56.5tn Revenues in FY25, up 1.1% YoY, and forming 100.8%/100.3% of our/consensus' FY25 Revenue estimate.

## 4Q25 EBITDA of Rp7.25tn (+13.7% YoY, +11.6% QoQ)
Relatively in line with our estimate. Cash opex increased 5.3% YoY in 4Q25, mainly driven by higher Cost of Services and Personnel expenses. To note, Cost of Services increased 3.9% YoY in 4Q25, while Personnel expenses increased 19.2% YoY in the same period. Meanwhile, Marketing expenses declined 12.0% YoY in 4Q25. Lastly, G&A expenses increased 25.5% YoY in the same period. As a result, EBITDA margin improved 191bps YoY/ 100bps QoQ to 47.2% in 4Q25. On a 12-month basis, IOH booked Rp26.6tn EBITDA in FY25, up 0.8% YoY, and forming 100.3%/99.8% of our/consensus' FY25 EBITDA estimate.

## D&A expenses
D&A expenses were flat YoY in 4Q25, possibly attributable to lower capex spending at 5.1% YoY in the same period. Operating Profit reached Rp3.30tn in 4Q25, up 35.9% YoY, beating our estimate by 5.2%, implying an operating margin increase to 21.5% in 4Q25 from 17.3% in 4Q24. On a 12-month basis, IOH booked Rp10.8tn Operating Profit in FY25, down 0.5% YoY, and forming 101.5%/102.4% of our/consensus' FY25 Operating Profit estimate.

## 4Q25 PATAMI of Rp1.92tn (+86.1% YoY, +53.5% QoQ)
Beat our estimate. IOH booked a 23.1% YoY non-operating expenses decline in 4Q25, mainly due to lower interest expenses and higher other income. To note, IOH booked Rp130bn other income in 4Q25 vs. Rp16bn other income in 4Q24, attributed to one-off gains from asset disposal. Meanwhile, interest expenses declined 13.4% YoY in 4Q25, despite booking an interest-bearing debt drawdown of Rp623bn on a QoQ basis. On a 12-month basis, IOH booked Rp5.51tn PATAMI in FY25, up 12.2% YoY, and forming 111.5%/113.5% of our/consensus' FY25 PATAMI estimate.

## Retain BUY
Strong earnings beat could be attributed to the lower D&A expenses, on a full year basis, and non-operating expenses (i.e. interest costs, income taxes, and minority interests). Nevertheless, IOH managed to book solid revenue growth, attributed to higher ARPU and solid data traffic growth, indicating solid operational performances likely due to purchasing power improvements. GPU-as-a-Service also started to deliver solid revenue as indicated by strong MIDI revenue adds on a QoQ basis. Overall, we saw an improvement in operational and financial performance, which we think can be sustained in 1H26.
