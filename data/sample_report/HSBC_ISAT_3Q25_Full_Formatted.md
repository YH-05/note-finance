## Executive Summary
*   **3Q results reaffirm mobile pricing discipline:** ARPU rises 4% q/q, but EBITDA growth is muted at 1% q/q.
*   **Earnings growth to improve:** Expected higher mobile revenue and growth in the enterprise segment.
*   **Maintain Buy:** Unchanged TP of IDR3,000; key catalysts are mobile revenue growth and fibre asset monetisation.

**Maintain Buy: earnings growth set to improve with higher mobile and enterprise revenue:**
We expect mobile revenue to rise due to higher mobile ARPU led by pricing discipline in the industry, removal of discounts and higher starter pack prices. Medium-term, we also expect the company to gain market share with the expansion of its network coverage and distribution, and increase its market share in the enterprise segment. Overall, we expect EBITDA / net profit CAGR of c5%/14% over 2024-27e. We see dividends rising progressively and expect the dividend payout ratio to rise to 70% by 2026e (from 55% in 2024). The stock is attractively valued at 2026e 3.7x EV/ EBITDA and 10.8x PE, with a 6.5% dividend yield. Our TP is unchanged at IDR3,000. See page 6 for risks to our view.

**3Q25 revenue in line; ARPU up c4% q/q:**
Revenue for the quarter grew c4% q/q to IDR14trn. Mobile revenue was up c4% q/q to IDR11.8trn. This was a result of higher ARPU driven by higher starter pack prices, rationalisation of freebies and discounts, and hyper personalisation initiatives. Mobile ARPU for 3Q25 stood at IDR40k (vs IDR38.5k in 2Q). EBITDA for the quarter was up c1% q/q to IDR6.5trn. EBITDA growth was slower q/q due to some costs booked upfront in the enterprise segment and volatility around some cost items. EBITDA for 9M’25 came at IDR19.3trn (down 3% y/y) and was tracking at 71% our 2025 estimate. EBITDA margin for 9M25 stood at 47.0% (vs 47.8% for 9M24). Net profit for 3Q stood at IDR1.25trn up 9% y/y.

**Key highlights from the earnings call:**
1.  The company reiterated its 2025 EBITDA growth guidance of low-single digit growth y/y.
2.  It expects mobile ARPU to trend upwards and expects ARPU to rise to IDR45-50k in the medium term.
3.  The bulk of revenue from GPU-as-a-service for 2025 should be reflected in 4Q25 (USD35m expected in 2025).
4.  It expects fibre asset monetisation to conclude during 4Q25.

## Valuation and Risks
*   **Methodology:** Discounted cash flow (FCFF).
*   **Assumptions:** WACC at 12.8%, Cost of equity at 14.6%, based on a risk-free rate of 4.25%, equity risk premium of 5.75%, beta of 1.8.

### Downside risks
*   Increase in competitive intensity leading to a lower level of data pricing and profitability.
*   Loss of market share as other peers are pursuing fixed-mobile convergence strategy.
*   Higher-than-expected spectrum cost leading to lower-than-expected margins.
*   Unfavourable regulatory changes.
