## Challenging Near-term Revenue Trends Weigh on Attractive Valuation

### CITI'S TAKE
Indosat disappointed in 2Q25 with NPAT contracting -12% QoQ / -29% YoY with 1H25 generating 40% of Street estimates. This miss was owing mainly on slow revenue growth trends with 2Q group revenues flat QoQ / -4% YoY with domestic consumption remaining soft. Guidance has been tempered with EBITDA growth now expected at low single digit levels from >10% previously. We reduce our FY25-26E recurring NPAT estimates 7%-9% and lower our TP to Rp2530 (from Rp2600). Indosat remains attractive at 4.7x FY25 EV/EBITDA. Near-term challenges owing to weak domestic consumption, however, may weigh down near-term share price prospects. Longer term, we expect market repair from a consolidated industry to aid in driving and reviving revenue growth.

**Revenue fails to improve in 2Q —** Group revenues disappointed at flat QoQ / -4% YoY. The softness in consumption sustained with mobile revenues (-1% QoQ / -5% YoY) weighing down overall group momentum, even against recent industry initiatives to raise starter pack pricing from ~Rp25k level to Rp35k uniformly by March 2025 as well as entry-level pre-paid top up price increases. Softness appears to be macro-driven given the absence of any competitive escalation within the industry, especially with XL-Smartfren consolidating in April 2025 and with mergers typically heralding a more rational competitive landscape. Subs base was essentially unchanged at 95.4mn. Blended ARPUs, however, declined ~2% QoQ even with a more benign competitive landscape and symptomatic of weaker consumer demand.

**Cost control helped offset the revenue softness —** Whilst revenues were soft, Indosat was able to sequentially improve EBITDA margins (+40bp QoQ) to 47.6%. The company booked double-digit QoQ decrease in personnel (-14% QoQ), marketing (-10% QoQ) and G&A spending (-10% QoQ). Do note that while Indosat’s reported profit dipped a sharp 22% QoQ, this was optically aggravated by one-off items booked in 1Q25 such as one-off tax reversals and gain on asset sales which had served to drive ~Rp0.3tn in non-recurring benefits in 1Q25.

**Guidance revision —** Indosat has retained its target to grow better-than-market on revenues. The key caveat, however, is that the overall market has not been growing with Indosat itself posting a -3% YoY contraction for 1H25. EBITDA growth guidance has been reduced from >10% to low single digit levels given the impact of a more problematic revenue momentum. Capex remains unchanged at ~Rp13tn. While revenues have admittedly fallen short in 1H25 (-3% YoY), we do see scope for some improvement in 2H with: (1) bulk of the US$35mn in GPUaaS revenues finally being booked; and (2) the recognition of back-ended benefits from starter pack pricing revisions in March and a 10% increase to the entry level pre-paid top-up pricing from June. Management has expressed confidence that it has started to see improvement in revenue in June/July and sees likely QoQ growth in revenue in 3Q25 and 4Q25.

**Implications —** We believe industry dynamics should eventually improve following the recent consolidation of XL & Smartfren which transforms Indonesia into a 3-player market. Traces of this are already evident with recent pricing strategies both on starter packs and top-ups. We trim FY25-26E recurring NPAT estimates by 7-9% reflecting a more cautious revenue outlook given near-term consumption softness. TP reduced to Rp2530 (from Rp2600). We keep Indosat at Buy given attractive valuation (4.7x/4.5x FY25/25 EV/EBITDA) and longer-term prospects with market consolidation. Near term, we see the street to be disappointed with the company’s earnings trajectory, Hence, we maintain our downside short-term view.

**Indosat**
**Valuation**
Our target price of Rp2,530 is based on an NPV-derived fair value on a FY25E DCF basis using a 10.2% WACC and a 2.0% terminal growth rate. Our WACC uses an 7.0% risk-free rate, 1.3x beta, and a 6.0% equity risk premium. At our target price, Indosat would trade at ~5x FY25E EV/EBITDA. Our model already incorporates potential capex/opex savings on the back of M&A synergies as well as expansion in fixed broadband contributions.

**Risks**
Downside risks that could cause Indosat shares to fail to reach our target price include: (1) weaker-than-expected cost savings post consolidation; (2) potential increased competitive intensity; and (3) potential for capex ramp-up with 5G & related spectrum needs.
